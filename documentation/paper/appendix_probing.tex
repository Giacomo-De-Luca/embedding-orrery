\section{Probe Configuration}
\label{app:probe-config}

All probes are trained \emph{in-platform} on the stored embedding vectors of a
collection (read from ChromaDB) against a chosen metadata field, with no model
inference: training is a few seconds of CPU. We expose three probe families.

\paragraph{Targets.}
A target field is either numeric or binary categorical. Numeric fields are used
directly. A categorical field with exactly two distinct non-null values is mapped
to $\{0,1\}$ deterministically (the lexicographically first value $\mapsto 0$; e.g.
\texttt{safe}$\mapsto 0$, \texttt{unsafe}$\mapsto 1$), following the two-class
setup of \citet{marks2023geometry}. Items whose target is null are excluded from
training but are still scored by the fitted probe. We require at least
$N_{\min}=50$ valid targets.

\paragraph{Train/validation split.}
For each field we draw a single random $80/20$ train/validation split
(seed $=42$) and reuse the \emph{same} split across all three probe families, so
their validation metrics are directly comparable. When more than $5\!\times\!10^{4}$
valid targets are present, the probe is trained on a fixed random subsample of that
size (all items are still scored). Inputs are cast to \texttt{float32}.

\paragraph{Ridge probe.}
$\ell_2$-regularised linear regression on per-feature standardised activations
($z$-scored using train-split statistics), $\alpha=1.0$. The learned direction
$\hat{\bm{\theta}}$ is the regression weight vector; every item is scored as
$s(\bm{x}) = \hat{\bm{\theta}}^{\top} \tilde{\bm{x}} + b$, where $\tilde{\bm{x}}$ is
the standardised activation and $b$ the fitted intercept.

\paragraph{Mass-mean probe.}
The closed-form difference-of-class-means direction of
\citet{marks2023geometry}, computed on standardised activations: with a median
split of the target,
$\hat{\bm{\theta}}_{\mathrm{mm}} = \bm{\mu}_{+} - \bm{\mu}_{-}$
(unit-normalised), where $\bm{\mu}_{\pm}$ are the mean activations of the
above-/below-median groups. Scores are the projection
$s(\bm{x}) = \hat{\bm{\theta}}_{\mathrm{mm}}^{\top}\tilde{\bm{x}}$. Because the
projection is uncalibrated, we report a calibrated $R^2$: we fit a univariate
least-squares readout $y \approx a\,s(\bm{x}) + c$ on the training split and
evaluate its $R^2$ on the validation split. (Standardising the activations makes
this a diagonal-covariance variant of the raw mass-mean probe; we do not apply the
full $\Sigma^{-1}$ correction, whose regularised form is subsumed by the ridge
probe.)

\paragraph{MLP probe.}
A nonlinear baseline: one hidden layer of width $256$ with ReLU and dropout $0.1$,
i.e. $\mathrm{Linear}(d,256)\to\mathrm{ReLU}\to\mathrm{Dropout}(0.1)\to
\mathrm{Linear}(256,1)$, trained with Adam (learning rate $10^{-3}$, weight decay
$10^{-4}$), mean-squared-error loss, batch size $64$, for up to $100$ epochs with
early stopping (patience $10$) on validation loss; the best-validation checkpoint
is restored. It has no explicit direction vector; scores are the network's
predictions. Training is seeded (seed $=42$) for reproducibility.

\paragraph{Reported metrics.}
For every (field, probe) we report the validation coefficient of determination
$R^2$ and Spearman rank correlation $\rho$ between predictions (or the mass-mean
projection) and the target; the underlying store additionally records Pearson $r$,
MSE, MAE, and the corresponding train-split values. Non-finite values arising from
degenerate targets are recorded as null rather than reported.
